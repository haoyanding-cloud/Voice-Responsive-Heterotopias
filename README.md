Introduction
This project is an interactive generative art installation developed on p5.js. By collecting the frequency and intensity of the viewer's voice in real time, the work drives thousands of particles to transform between "memory fragments" and "complete pictures". The audience's voice is not only a medium of interaction, but also a "developer" that salvages the broken memories deep in the subconscious. Through this real-time feedback, the work constructs a heterotopia space that is constantly collapsing and reorganized, sustained by sound.
Concept and Background Research
Concept:
The work is inspired by reflections on the physical properties of "memory". Memories are not static photos, but a pile of fragments ready to run away. In this project, I process images into fragmented particles, simulating trivial past events lurking in the depths of my mind.
Sound is the switch of memory: Many times, a melody or a sudden sound will instantly awaken a dusty past. What I want to say is that only when we give "energy" (sound) will memories emerge from the chaos and piece together a complete picture.
Fragile Entity: The completeness of this memory is transient and fragile. As the sound fades, these particles dissipate back into the darkness. This dynamic process of "patchwork and collapse" accurately captures the trance of people's memories.
Background Research:
The work is heavily influenced by the visual aesthetics of Dreamcore and Liminal Space. This aesthetic emphasizes "familiarity and alienation", using overexposed picture quality, afterimages of VHS tapes, and empty abandoned scenes to create a surreal dreaminess. At the same time, in terms of interaction logic, it refers to the force field systems in computational art to explore how to use code to simulate the randomness and sense of order in nature.
The concept of "Heterotopia" was proposed by the philosopher Michel Foucault. It refers to those "alternative Spaces" that truly exist but are completely different from everyday Spaces.
Heterotopia often exist between "this shore" and "the other shore".
What I want is to put people on the verge of memory and forgetting, a typical heterotopian psychological space.
My work is a "sound-driven Heterotopia". It is like a mirror. Although it is a real interactive system, the picture it presents is a fragmented memory space-time that we cannot enter. In this space, sound is the only "pass". When the sound fades away, this space disintegrates. I borrow Foucault's theory to explore how technology can help us build an alternative place to house emotions and the subconscious. Because heterotopia is essentially a spiritual utopia created by humans to accommodate those emotions that cannot be placed in reality.
